
Input0: A coil with $100$ turns experiences a change in flux of $0.02\,\mathrm{Wb}$ in $0.5\,\mathrm{s}$. Induced emf?
 ----- Reference Model ----- Reference Response: The induced emf is given by the formula
$$\varepsilon=-\frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$$
where $\Delta\Phi$ is the change in flux and $\Delta t$ is the time interval over which the change occurs.
In this case, $\Delta\Phi=0.02\,\mathrm{Wb}$ and $\Delta t=0.5\,\mathrm{s}$, so the induced emf is
$$\varepsilon=-\frac{0.02\,\mathrm{Wb}}{0.5\,\mathrm{s}}=-0.04\,\mathrm{V}$$
This means that the induced emf is $-0.04\,\mathrm{V}$, which is a negative value. This is because the induced emf is in the opposite direction to the change in flux.
 ----- Policy Model ----- Policy Response: Step 1:\\$e=-N\frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}=-100\times\frac{0.02}{0.5}=-2\,\mathrm{V}$.\\Answer:\\$-2\,\mathrm{V}$.
 ----- Expected Response ----- Expected Answer: Step 1:\\$\varepsilon= -N\,\frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$.\\Step 2:\\$= -100\times \frac{0.02}{0.5}= -100\times 0.04= -4\,\mathrm{V}$. Magnitude $4\,\mathrm{V}$.\\Answer:\\$4\,\mathrm{V}$.================================================================================

Input1: A soap film ($n=1.33$) reflects violet light ($400\,\mathrm{nm}$) most strongly. What is the minimum thickness of the film for constructive interference?
 ----- Reference Model ----- Reference Response: The wavelength of the light in the film is given by
$$\lambda = \frac{\lambda_0}{n} = \frac{400\,\mathrm{nm}}{1.33} = 300\,\mathrm{nm}$$
The thickness of the film must be an integer multiple of half the wavelength, so the minimum thickness is
$$d = \frac{\lambda}{2} = \frac{300\,\mathrm{nm}}{2} = 150\,\mathrm{nm}$$
 ----- Policy Model ----- Policy Response: Step 1:\\$\lambda_{\text{air}}=2d\,n\sin\theta$. For $\theta=90^{\circ}$, $\lambda_{\text{air}}=2d\,n$.\\Step 2:\\$\lambda_{\text{air}}=400\,\mathrm{nm}/1.33=2d\,1.33$.\\Step 3:\\$d=400\,\mathrm{nm}/2.66=150\,\mathrm{nm}$.\\Answer:\\$150\,\mathrm{nm}$.
 ----- Expected Response ----- Expected Answer: Step 1:\\For a film with air on both sides, the reflection at the top interface introduces a half-wavelength phase shift. The condition for the first constructive maximum is $2 n t = (m + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$ with $m = 0$.\\Step 2:\\$2 \times 1.33 \times t = 0.5 \times 400\,\mathrm{nm} \to 2.66 t = 200\,\mathrm{nm} \to t \approx 75.2\,\mathrm{nm}$.\\Answer:\\The minimum film thickness is about $75\,\mathrm{nm}$.================================================================================

Input2: A $1\,\mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor and a $2\,\mu\mathrm{F}$ capacitor are in parallel. Total capacitance?
 ----- Reference Model ----- Reference Response: capacitance of two capacitors in parallel is the sum of the individual capacitances. So, the total capacitance is $3\,\mu\mathrm{F}$.
 ----- Policy Model ----- Policy Response: Step 1:\\$C_{\text{total}}=C_1+C_2=\frac{1\times 2}{1+2}=1/3\,\mu\mathrm{F}$.\\Step 2:\\(C_{\text{total}})^{-1}=(C_1)^{-1}+(C_2)^{-1}=3\,\mu\mathrm{F}^{-1}$.\\Step 3:\\Hence, $C_{\text{total}}=1/3\,\mu\mathrm{F}$.\\Answer:\\$1/3\,\mu\mathrm{F}$.
 ----- Expected Response ----- Expected Answer: Step 1:\\Parallel: $C_{\mathrm{total}} = C_{1} + C_{2} = 1 + 2 = 3\,\mu\mathrm{F}$.\\Answer:\\$3\,\mu\mathrm{F}$.================================================================================

Input3: A spaceship moves at $0.95c$. If it fires a laser beam forward, what speed does an Earth observer measure for the laser?
 ----- Reference Model ----- Reference Response: The speed of light is $c$ in all inertial frames. The speed of the spaceship is $0.95c$ in the spaceship's frame. The speed of the laser is $c$ in the spaceship's frame. The speed of the laser is $0.95c$ in the Earth frame.
 ----- Policy Model ----- Policy Response: Step 1:\\relativistic velocity addition formula: $u' = \frac{u+v}{1+uv/c^2}$\\Step 2:\\$u$ is the speed of the laser relative to the spaceship, $v$ is the speed of the spaceship relative to Earth, $u'$ is the speed of the laser relative to Earth.\\Step 3:\\$u = c$, $v = 0.95c$, $u' =?$\\Step 4:\\$u' = \frac{c+0.95c}{1+(0.95)(c/c)} = \frac{1.95c}{1.95} = 0.95c$\\Answer:\\$0.95c$
 ----- Expected Response ----- Expected Answer: Step 1:\\By the principle of invariance, the speed of light is always $c$ in any inertial frame.\\Step 2:\\Thus, the Earth observer measures the laser at $c = 3 \times 10^{8}\,\mathrm{m/s}$.\\Answer:\\The laser travels at $3 \times 10^{8}\,\mathrm{m/s}$.================================================================================
